Wishbone

Backbone App using Best Buy API

Wishbone is a Backbone application that was made in three weeks for The Iron Yard's final-project demonstration. My goal was to develop an application that helped users keep track of the products that they want but also keep track of the products that their friends are interested in.

    The assignment required that apps:
  • used an MVC framework
  • communicated with at least one HTTP API
  • featured CRUD functionality
  • included User Authorization and Authentication
  • adapted repsonsively to mobile and desktop
  • showcased the ability to plan and execute the production of a Front-End application

Three Broomsticks

Being a Harry Potter fan, I wanted to bring a personalized twist to the assignment of making a restaurant app with cart and checkout funcitonality. This was made over the weekend after learning Backbone and gave me practice using Parse as a back-end and Jquery to toggle classes.

Greenville Parking

Group Hackathon Backbone Parking App

Greenville Parking was created in a span of 18 hours for the Imagine Upstate Hackathon in Greenville, SC. Our group chose to tackle the problem of finding and paying for private parking. Using Greenville Parking, businesses can register a private lot, attendants can monitor the capacity, and users can find private lots close to their destination. I took on the business registration side, providing businesses with CRUD functionality.
